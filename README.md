1. How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program?
How does creating “user stories” help with this?

Interpreting user needs begins with understanding what user is trying to accomplish and why it matters.
User stories help translate those needs into clear, actionable requirements by framing them from the 
users perspective. The "As a...., I want...., so that..." format ensures that development focuses
on delivering value rather than just building fetures. User stories also help break down complex needs
into smaller tasks that can be implemented, tested and refined iteratively

3. What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?

Being a good team member means communicating clearly, collaborating effectively and supporting the team's
shared goals. It involves being accountable for your work being open to feedback, and helping remove
obstacles that affect the team's progress. Good team members contribute to a positive enrionment
by being reliable, repsectful and committed to delivering high quality work that aligns with the
team;s definition of done.
